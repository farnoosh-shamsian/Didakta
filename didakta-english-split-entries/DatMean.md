# §DatMean. Manner and means

The dative, usually of an abstract noun, may indicate accompanying circumstance and manner or the means of an action. The datives of manner, means and attendant circumstances are sometimes similar to adverbs, for instance: [βίᾳ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=bi%2Fa%7C&la=greek&can=bi%2Fa%7C1&prior=dro/mw|) by force, [δίκῃ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=di%2Fkh%7C&la=greek&can=di%2Fkh%7C0&prior=bi/a|) justly, [δόλῳ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=do%2Flw%7C&la=greek&can=do%2Flw%7C0&prior=di/kh|) by craft, ([τῷ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=tw%3D%7C&la=greek&can=tw%3D%7C1&prior=do/lw|)) [ἔργῳ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%29%2Frgw%7C&la=greek&can=e%29%2Frgw%7C0&prior=tw=|) in fact, [ἡσυχῇ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=h%28suxh%3D%7C&la=greek&can=h%28suxh%3D%7C0&prior=e\)/rgw|) quietly, [σπουδῇ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=spoudh%3D%7C&la=greek&can=spoudh%3D%7C0&prior=siwph=|) hastily, [τῇ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=th%3D%7C&la=greek&can=th%3D%7C0&prior=spoudh=|) [ἀληθείᾳ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=a%29lhqei%2Fa%7C&la=greek&can=a%29lhqei%2Fa%7C0&prior=th=|) in truth, [τῷ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=tw%3D%7C&la=greek&can=tw%3D%7C3&prior=a\)lhqei/a|) [ὄντι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=o%29%2Fnti&la=greek&can=o%29%2Fnti0&prior=tw=|) in reality, [ὀργῇ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=o%29rgh%3D%7C&la=greek&can=o%29rgh%3D%7C0&prior=o\)/nti) in anger.
