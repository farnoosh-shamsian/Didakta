# §PerfInten. Intensive Perfect[^76]

The perfects tense with atelic verbs[^77] may indicate an emphasis of the degree of the state expressed by the verb. These are often called intensive perfects. Such are: verbs of the *senses* ([δέδορκα](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=de%2Fdorka&la=greek&can=de%2Fdorka0&prior=oi\)=da) gaze, [πέφρικα](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=pe%2Ffri_ka&la=greek&can=pe%2Ffri_ka0&prior=de/dorka) shudder), of *sustained sound* ([κέκραγα](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ke%2Fkra_ga&la=greek&can=ke%2Fkra_ga0&prior=pe/fri_ka) bawl, [λέληκα](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=le%2Flhka&la=greek&can=le%2Flhka0&prior=ke/kra_ga) shout, [βέβρυχα](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=be%2Fbru_xa&la=greek&can=be%2Fbru_xa0&prior=le/lhka) roar), of *emotion* ([πεφόβημαι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=pefo%2Fbhmai&la=greek&can=pefo%2Fbhmai0&prior=be/bru_xa) am filled with alarm, [γέγηθα](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ge%2Fghqa&la=greek&can=ge%2Fghqa0&prior=pefo/bhmai) am glad, [μέμηλε](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=me%2Fmhle&la=greek&can=me%2Fmhle0&prior=ge/ghqa) cares for), of *gesture* ([κέχηνα](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ke%2Fxhna&la=greek&can=ke%2Fxhna0&prior=me/mhle) keep the mouth agape), and many others ([σεσίγηκα](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=sesi%2F_ghka&la=greek&can=sesi%2F_ghka0&prior=ke/xhna) am still, etc.).
