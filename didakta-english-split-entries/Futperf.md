# §Futperf. Future Completed Action[^83]

The future perfect denotes a future state resulting from a completed action: [ἀναγεγράψομαι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=a%29nagegra%2Fyomai&la=greek&can=a%29nagegra%2Fyomai0&prior=kataskoph/n) I shall stand enrolled, [δεδήσεται](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=dedh%2Fsetai&la=greek&can=dedh%2Fsetai0&prior=a\)nagegra/yomai) he shall be kept in prison. 

→ “[ἡ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=h%28&la=greek&can=h%2810&prior=dedh/setai) [θύρα](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=qu%2Fra_&la=greek&can=qu%2Fra_0&prior=h\() [**κεκλῄσεται**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=keklh%2F%7Csetai&la=greek&can=keklh%2F%7Csetai0&prior=qu/ra_)” [**Ar. Lys. 1071**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Aristoph.%20Lys.%201071&lang=original); ⇾ the door will be kept shut

→ “[σὲ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=se%5C&la=greek&can=se%5C0&prior=qnh/|skw) [δ᾽](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=d%27&la=greek&can=d%276&prior=se\\) [ἄλλη](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=a%29%2Fllh&la=greek&can=a%29%2Fllh0&prior=d%27) [τις](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=tis&la=greek&can=tis2&prior=a\)/llh) [γυνὴ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=gunh%5C&la=greek&can=gunh%5C1&prior=tis) [**κεκτήσεται**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=kekth%2Fsetai&la=greek&can=kekth%2Fsetai0&prior=gunh\\)” [**Eur. Alc. 181**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus%3Atext%3A1999.01.0087%3Acard%3D136); ⇾ Some other woman will possess you

Most future perfects are middle in form, passive in meaning. The active future perfect is usually periphrastic, meaning that it is formed by combining the perfect active participle with [ἔσομαι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%29%2Fsomai&la=greek&can=e%29%2Fsomai0) shall be; for instance, [γεγραφὼς](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=gegrafw%5Cs&la=greek&can=gegrafw%5Cs0&prior=e\)/somai) [ἔσομαι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%29%2Fsomai&la=greek&can=e%29%2Fsomai1&prior=gegrafw\\s) I shall have written. Exceptions are two perfects with a present meaning, [ἕστηκα](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%28%2Fsthka&la=greek&can=e%28%2Fsthka0) I stand ([ἵστημι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=i%28%2Fsthmi&la=greek&can=i%28%2Fsthmi0&prior=e\(/sthka) set) and [τέθνηκα](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=te%2Fqnhka&la=greek&can=te%2Fqnhka0&prior=i\(/sthmi) I am dead ([θνῄσκω](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=qnh%2F%7Cskw&la=greek&can=qnh%2F%7Cskw0&prior=te/qnhka)), which form the future perfects [ἑστήξω](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%28sth%2Fcw&la=greek&can=e%28sth%2Fcw0&prior=qnh/|skw) I shall stand, [τεθνήξω](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=teqnh%2Fcw&la=greek&can=teqnh%2Fcw0&prior=e\(sth/cw) I shall be dead.

→ “[τὰ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ta%5C&la=greek&can=ta%5C4&prior=keklh/|setai) [δέοντ᾽](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=de%2Font%27&la=greek&can=de%2Font%270&prior=ta\\) [**ἐσόμεθα**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%29so%2Fmeqa&la=greek&can=e%29so%2Fmeqa0&prior=de/ont%27) **[ἐγνωκότες](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%29gnwko%2Ftes&la=greek&can=e%29gnwko%2Ftes0&prior=e\)so/meqa)**” [**D. 4.50**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Dem.%204.50&lang=original); ⇾ we shall have determined on our duty
