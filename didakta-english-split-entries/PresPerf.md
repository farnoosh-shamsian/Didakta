# §PresPerf. Resultative and Perfective Present

The present of certain verbs often expresses an enduring result, and may be translated by the perfect: [ἀδικῶ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=a%29dikw%3D&la=greek&can=a%29dikw%3D0&prior=h\(/kw) I am guilty ([ἄδικός](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=a%29%2Fdiko%2Fs&la=greek&can=a%29%2Fdiko%2Fs0&prior=a\)dikw=) [εἰμι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ei%29mi&la=greek&can=ei%29mi0&prior=a\)/diko/s)), *I have done wrong*, [νικῶ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ni_kw%3D&la=greek&can=ni_kw%3D0&prior=ei\)mi), [κρατῶ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=kratw%3D&la=greek&can=kratw%3D0&prior=ni_kw=), I am victorious, I have conquered, [ἡττῶμαι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=h%28ttw%3Dmai&la=greek&can=h%28ttw%3Dmai0&prior=kratw=) I am conquered, [φεύγω](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=feu%2Fgw&la=greek&can=feu%2Fgw0&prior=h\(ttw=mai) I am the defendant or I am an exile, [προδίδωμι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=prodi%2Fdwmi&la=greek&can=prodi%2Fdwmi0&prior=feu/gontes) I am a traitor, [ἁλίσκομαι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=a%28li%2Fskomai&la=greek&can=a%28li%2Fskomai0&prior=prodi/dwmi) I am captured, [στέρομαι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ste%2Fromai&la=greek&can=ste%2Fromai0&prior=a\(li/skomai) I am deprived, [γίγνομαι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=gi%2Fgnomai&la=greek&can=gi%2Fgnomai0&prior=ste/romai) I am a descendant.

The perfective present is common with verbs of hearing, saying, learning, whose action commenced in the past, but whose effect continues into the present. The present tense with telic verbs has a resultative meaning, such as [ἥκω](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=h%28%2Fkw&la=greek&can=h%28%2Fkw0&prior=a\)/rti) I am come, I have arrived, [οἴχομαι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=oi%29%2Fxomai&la=greek&can=oi%29%2Fxomai0&prior=h\(/kw) I am gone, etc. The present may also indicate an action begun in the past and continued in the present. This use of present tense is accompanied by a definite or indefinite expression of past time.
