# §ImpfComp. Imperfect of Completed Action[^58]

The imperfect with [telic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telicity) verbs[^59] draws attention toward the consequences of the action, while the present of these verbs suggests that the action is not completed yet. Thus, [ἧκον](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=h%28%3Dkon&la=greek&can=h%28%3Dkon0&prior=dei=cai) I had come (rarely I came), [ᾠχόμην](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=w%29%7Cxo%2Fmhn&la=greek&can=w%29%7Cxo%2Fmhn0&prior=h\(=kon) I had departed, as [ἐνίκων](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%29ni%2F_kwn&la=greek&can=e%29ni%2F_kwn0&prior=w\)|xo/mhn) I was victorious, [ἡττώμην](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=h%28ttw%2Fmhn&la=greek&can=h%28ttw%2Fmhn0&prior=e\)ni/_kwn) I was defeated.
