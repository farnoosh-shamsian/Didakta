# §MidIndir. Indirect Reflexive Middle[^86]

In the indirect reflexive middle, the subject of the verb is affected by the verb, often as a beneficiary. The Indirect Reflexive Middle represents the subject as acting *for himself, with reference to himself*, or *with something belonging to himself*. *Self* is often the indirect object. So [πορίζεσθαι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=pori%2Fzesqai&la=greek&can=pori%2Fzesqai0&prior=a\)po/llusqai) provide for oneself ([πορίζειν](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=pori%2Fzein&la=greek&can=pori%2Fzein0&prior=pori/zesqai) provide), [φυλάττεσθαι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=fula%2Fttesqai&la=greek&can=fula%2Fttesqai0&prior=pori/zein) guard against ([φυλάττειν](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=fula%2Fttein&la=greek&can=fula%2Fttein0&prior=fula/ttesqai) keep guard), [αἱρεῖσθαι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ai%28rei%3Dsqai&la=greek&can=ai%28rei%3Dsqai0&prior=fula/ttein) choose (take for oneself). The middle often denotes that the subject acts with something belonging to himself (material objects, means, powers). It is often used for acts done willingly.
