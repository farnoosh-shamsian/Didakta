# §FutJuss. Jussive Future[^64]

The future in the second person may function similar to the imperative, and express an urgent command, prohibition, concession or permission. The tone of the jussive future is generally familiar. In combination with [οὐ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ou%29&la=greek&can=ou%291&prior=dra/_somen) [μή](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=mh%2F&la=greek&can=mh%2F1&prior=ste/rcw) , it indicates urgent prohibition. This use of the future tense is post-homeric.
