# §Fut. Future Events or Expectations

The future denotes an action that will take place at some future time. The future tense indicates that according to the opinion, expectation, hope, fear, or purpose of the speaker or the agent, the action would happen in the future. Depending on the context, the action of the future could be both continuative (like the present) or, similar to the aorist, express simple attainment. For instance, [πείσω](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=pei%2Fsw&la=greek&can=pei%2Fsw0&prior=ta/lanton) means I shall try to persuade, or I shall convince (resultative), [βασιλεύσω](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=basileu%2Fsw&la=greek&can=basileu%2Fsw0&prior=pei/sw) I shall be king, shall reign or I shall become king (ingressive).
