# §OptPoten. Potential Optative[^107]

The potential optative with [ἄν](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=a%29%2Fn&la=greek&can=a%29%2Fn32&prior=a\)/n) states a future possibility, propriety, or likelihood. The potential optative, which in Attic regularly takes [ἄν](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=a%29%2Fn&la=greek&can=a%29%2Fn28&prior=h\(goi=to), is occasionally found in Homer and later poetry in an earlier form, without that particle. The potential optative is sometimes used to soften the statement of an opinion or fact, or to express irony.
