# §FutNoni. Non-indicative[^65]

The tenses of the moods other than the indicative rarely have relative temporal value, however, they express the grammatical aspect of the verb. The aspect of a verb indicates the way an action extends over time and the way it is presented. For instance, It may present an action as a complete whole, or an incomplete, ongoing process. The future stem does not have an aspectual value, meaning that it is neutral and makes no distinction between completed or imperfect actions. The future participle refers to an action posterior to the main verb.

With participles: The future participle is often voluntative and expresses purpose. In combination with [ὡς](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=w%28s&la=greek&can=w%28s0&prior=sunh/lqomen), it indicates the intention of the subject. 

→ “[οὐ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ou%29&la=greek&can=ou%290&prior=w\)/|khsan) [συνήλθομεν](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=sunh%2Flqomen&la=greek&can=sunh%2Flqomen0&prior=ou\)) [ὡς](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=w%28s&la=greek&can=w%28s0&prior=sunh/lqomen) [βασιλεῖ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=basilei%3D&la=greek&can=basilei%3D0&prior=w\(s) [**πολεμήσοντες**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=polemh%2Fsontes&la=greek&can=polemh%2Fsontes0&prior=basilei=)” [**X. A. 2.3.21**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Xen.%20Anab.%202.3.21&lang=original); ⇾ we have not come together for the purpose of waging war with the king

→ “[παρεσκευάζοντο](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=pareskeua%2Fzonto&la=greek&can=pareskeua%2Fzonto0&prior=*\)aqhnai=oi) [ὡς](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=w%28s&la=greek&can=w%28s0&prior=pareskeua/zonto) [**πολεμήσοντες**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=polemh%2Fsontes&la=greek&can=polemh%2Fsontes0&prior=w\(s)” [**T. 2.7.1**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Thuc.+2.7.1&fromdoc=Perseus%3Atext%3A1999.01.0247); ⇾ they prepared themselves for wage war

The verb [ἔρχομαι](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%29%2Frxomai&la=greek&can=e%29%2Frxomai0) with future participle is idiomatic, and often translates to “about to” or “going to”.

→ “ [ἐγὼ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%29gw%5C&la=greek&can=e%29gw%5C0&prior=le/gousi) [δὲ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=de%5C&la=greek&can=de%5C0&prior=e\)gw\\) [περὶ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=peri%5C&la=greek&can=peri%5C0&prior=de\\) [μὲν](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=me%5Cn&la=greek&can=me%5Cn0&prior=peri\\) [τούτων](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=tou%2Ftwn&la=greek&can=tou%2Ftwn0&prior=me\\n) [οὐκ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ou%29k&la=greek&can=ou%29k0&prior=tou/twn) [**ἔρχομαι**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%29%2Frxomai&la=greek&can=e%29%2Frxomai0&prior=ou\)k) **[ἐρέων](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%29re%2Fwn&la=greek&can=e%29re%2Fwn0&prior=e\)/rxomai)** [ὡς](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=w%28s&la=greek&can=w%28s0&prior=e\)re/wn) …” [**Hdt. 1.5.3**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Hdt.+1.5.3&fromdoc=Perseus%3Atext%3A1999.01.0126); ⇾ About these matters, I am not going to say that ...

With infinitives: When the context shows that stress is laid on the idea of futurity, the future infinitive, referring to future time relative to the main verb, is sometimes used instead of the present or aorist.
