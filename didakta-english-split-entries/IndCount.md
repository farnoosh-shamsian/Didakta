# §IndCount. Counterfactual Indicative

The indicative of the historical tenses with [ἄν](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=a%29%2Fn&la=greek&can=a%29%2Fn9&prior=a\)/n) ([κέν](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ke%2Fn&la=greek&can=ke%2Fn1&prior=a\)/n)) may indicate counterfactual (or unreal) actions. The imperfect refers to the present or the past, the aorist to the past (rarely to the present), the pluperfect to the present (less commonly to the past). This use of the indicative with [ἄν](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=a%29%2Fn&la=greek&can=a%29%2Fn10&prior=*g) undicating unreality is closely connected to past potential use of the indicative. The past potential use of the indicative with [ἄν](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=a%29%2Fn&la=greek&can=a%29%2Fn5&prior=a\)/n) ([κέν](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ke%2Fn&la=greek&can=ke%2Fn0&prior=a\)/n)) expresses past potentiality, probability (cautious statement), or necessity.
