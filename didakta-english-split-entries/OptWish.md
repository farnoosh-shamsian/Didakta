# §OptWish. Optative of Wish[^105]

The optative without [ἄν](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=a%29%2Fn&la=greek&can=a%29%2Fn27&prior=a\)/n) is used to express a wish referring to the future.  (negative [μή](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=mh%2F&la=greek&can=mh%2F15&prior=a\)/n)). The optative of wish, or cupitive optative, is often introduced by [εἰ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ei%29&la=greek&can=ei%293&prior=dwrodokh/santa) [γάρ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ga%2Fr&la=greek&can=ga%2Fr3&prior=ei\)), [εἴθε](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ei%29%2Fqe&la=greek&can=ei%29%2Fqe3&prior=ga/r) (Hom. [αἰ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ai%27&la=greek&can=ai%270&prior=ei\)/qe) [γάρ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ga%2Fr&la=greek&can=ga%2Fr4&prior=ai%27), [αἴθε](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ai%29%2Fqe&la=greek&can=ai%29%2Fqe1&prior=ga/r)), or by [εἰ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ei%29&la=greek&can=ei%294&prior=ai\)/qe), [ὡς](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=w%28s&la=greek&can=w%28s2&prior=ei\)) (both poetical).

→ “[ὦ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=w%29%3D&la=greek&can=w%29%3D0&prior=mh/) [παῖ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=pai%3D&la=greek&can=pai%3D0&prior=w\)=), [**γένοιο**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ge%2Fnoio&la=greek&can=ge%2Fnoio0&prior=pai=) [πατρὸς](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=patro%5Cs&la=greek&can=patro%5Cs0&prior=ge/noio) [εὐτυχέστερος](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=eu%29tuxe%2Fsteros&la=greek&can=eu%29tuxe%2Fsteros0&prior=patro\\s)” [**S. Aj. 550**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Soph.%20Aj.%20550&lang=original); ⇾ ah, boy, mayest thou prove more fortunate than thy sire

→ “[ὡς](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=w%28s&la=greek&can=w%28s3&prior=ge/noito) [**ὄλοιτο**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=o%29%2Floito&la=greek&can=o%29%2Floito0&prior=w\(s)” [**S. El. 126**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Soph.%20El.%20126&lang=original); ⇾ may he perish

→ “[εἴθε](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ei%29%2Fqe&la=greek&can=ei%29%2Fqe4&prior=ga/r) [φίλος](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=fi%2Flos&la=greek&can=fi%2Flos0&prior=ei\)/qe) [ἡμῖν](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=h%28mi%3Dn&la=greek&can=h%28mi%3Dn0&prior=fi/los) [**γένοιο**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ge%2Fnoio&la=greek&can=ge%2Fnoio1&prior=h\(mi=n)” [**X. H. 4.1.38**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Xen.%20Hell.%204.1.38&lang=original); ⇾ oh, if you would become our friend

The optative introduced by [εἰ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ei%29&la=greek&can=ei%296&prior=w\(s) [γάρ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ga%2Fr&la=greek&can=ga%2Fr5&prior=ei\)), etc. is sometimes explained as a protasis with the conclusion omitted: 

→ “[εἰ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ei%29&la=greek&can=ei%295&prior=w\(s) [γὰρ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ga%5Cr&la=greek&can=ga%5Cr2&prior=ei\)) [**γένοιτο**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ge%2Fnoito&la=greek&can=ge%2Fnoito1&prior=ga\\r)” [**X. C. 6.1.38**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Xen.%20Cyrop.%206.1.38&lang=original); ⇾ would that it might happen

An unattainable wish, referring to the present, may be expressed by the present optative in Homer:

→ “[εἴθ᾽](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=ei%29%2Fq%27&la=greek&can=ei%29%2Fq%271&prior=ge/noio) [**ἡβώοιμι**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=h%28bw%2Foimi&la=greek&can=h%28bw%2Foimi0&prior=ei\)/q%27)” [**Hom. Il. 23.624**](https://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus%3Atext%3A1999.01.0133%3Abook%3D23%3Acard%3D624); ⇾ would that I were young again

Under wishes are included execrations and protestations: 

→ “[**ἐξολοίμην**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%29coloi%2Fmhn&la=greek&can=e%29coloi%2Fmhn0&prior=po/lin)” [**Ar. Ach. 324**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Aristoph.%20Ach.%20324&lang=original); ⇾ may I perish

The optative may indicate rhetorical wishes, willingness, or indifferance as in imprecations.

Il. 2.340**](https://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus%3Atext%3A1999.01.0133%3Abook%3D2%3Acard%3D340); ⇾ into the fire let us cast all counsels and plans of warriors
