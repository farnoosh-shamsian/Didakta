# §AutRefl. Reflexive[^131]

Instead of the indirect [ἑαυτοῦ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%28autou%3D&la=greek&can=e%28autou%3D0), etc., the oblique cases of [αὐτός](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=au%29to%2Fs&la=greek&can=au%29to%2Fs0&prior=e\(autou=) may be used as a reflexive pronoun:

→ “[ἐπειρᾶτο](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%29peira%3Dto&la=greek&can=e%29peira%3Dto0&prior=au\)to/s) [τοὺς](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=tou%5Cs&la=greek&can=tou%5Cs0&prior=e\)peira=to) [Ἀθηναίους](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=*%29aqhnai%2Fous&la=greek&can=*%29aqhnai%2Fous0&prior=tou\\s) [τῆς](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=th%3Ds&la=greek&can=th%3Ds0&prior=*\)aqhnai/ous) [ἐς](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%29s&la=greek&can=e%29s0&prior=th=s) [**αὐτὸν**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=au%29to%5Cn&la=greek&can=au%29to%5Cn0&prior=e\)s) [ὀργῆς](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=o%29rgh%3Ds&la=greek&can=o%29rgh%3Ds0&prior=au\)to\\n) [παραλύειν](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=paralu%2F_ein&la=greek&can=paralu%2F_ein0&prior=o\)rgh=s)” [**T. 2.65**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Thuc.%202.65&lang=original); ⇾ he tried to divert the Athenians from their anger against himself

When [ἑαυτοῦ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%28autou%3D&la=greek&can=e%28autou%3D1&prior=paralu/_ein), etc. precede, [αὐτοῦ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=au%29tou%3D&la=greek&can=au%29tou%3D0&prior=e\(autou=), etc. are usual instead of the direct reflexive: 

→ “[τὴν](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=th%5Cn&la=greek&can=th%5Cn0&prior=au\)tou=) [ἑαυτοῦ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%28autou%3D&la=greek&can=e%28autou%3D2&prior=th\\n) [γνώμην](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=gnw%2Fmhn&la=greek&can=gnw%2Fmhn0&prior=e\(autou=) [ἀπεφαίνετο](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=a%29pefai%2Fneto&la=greek&can=a%29pefai%2Fneto0&prior=gnw/mhn) [Σωκράτης](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=*swkra%2Fths&la=greek&can=*swkra%2Fths0&prior=a\)pefai/neto) [πρὸς](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=pro%5Cs&la=greek&can=pro%5Cs0&prior=*swkra/ths) [τοὺς](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=tou%5Cs&la=greek&can=tou%5Cs1&prior=pro\\s) [ὁμιλοῦντας](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=o%28mi_lou%3Dntas&la=greek&can=o%28mi_lou%3Dntas0&prior=tou\\s) [**αὐτῷ**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=au%29tw%3D%7C&la=greek&can=au%29tw%3D%7C0&prior=o\(mi_lou=ntas)” [**X. M. 4.7.1**](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Xen.%20Mem.%204.7.1&lang=original); ⇾ Socrates was wont to set forth his opinion to those who conversed with him”

# **Notes** {#notes}

### **Agr. Agreement[^132]**

This tag is applied when a word has been assigned a certain case to correspond with another word, such as in attributive adjectives or appositives.

### 

### **GBP. Governed by Preposition**

This tag is used to annotate words in the oblique cases that are governed by an adposition. In previous versions of Didakta, different uses of adpositions were also categorized, however, due to the complexity of the topic the the sections were eventually omitted.
