# §MidRecip. Reciprocal Middle[^91]

Reciprocal middle verbs involve events that naturally need more than one participant, acting toward each other in the same manner, meaning that the relation between each participant to the other one is the same; for instance, with verbs of contending, conversing (questioning, replying), *greeting*, *embracing*, etc. The reciprocal middle is often found with compounds of [διά](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=dia%2F&la=greek&can=dia%2F0&prior=kateskeua/sato).
