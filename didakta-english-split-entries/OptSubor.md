# §OptSubor. In subordinate clause[^108]

The optative mood is frequently used in different types of subordinate clause, including temporal, conditional or relative clauses. In subordinate clauses in the secondary or historic sequence, the optative may replace the original mood. This use of optative is limited to the subordinate clauses of the secondary sequence (imperfect, aorist, pluperfect) and does not appear in primary sequence (present, perfect, future) and is called the oblique optative. The indefinite subordinate clauses of historic sequence may use the iterative optative (without [ἄν](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=a%29%2Fn&la=greek&can=a%29%2Fn28&prior=h\(goi=to)) to express a habitual or repeated action.
