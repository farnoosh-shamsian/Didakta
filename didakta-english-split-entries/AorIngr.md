# §AorIngr. Ingressive Aorist[^67]

The aorist of verbs whose present denotes a state or a continued action, expresses the entrance into that state or the beginning of that action. The ingressive aorist is used with atelic verbs[^68], particularly with stative verbs and indicates the beginning of a state; for instance, [ἄρχω](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=a%29%2Frxw&la=greek&can=a%29%2Frxw0&prior=boulh=|) rule: [ἦρξα](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=h%29%3Drca&la=greek&can=h%29%3Drca0&prior=a\)/rxw) became ruler, [βασιλεύω](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=basileu%2Fw&la=greek&can=basileu%2Fw0&prior=h\)=rca) am king, rule: [ἐβασίλευσα](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%29basi%2Fleusa&la=greek&can=e%29basi%2Fleusa0&prior=basileu/w) became king, ascended the throne, or [νοσῶ](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=nosw%3D&la=greek&can=nosw%3D0&prior=e\)qa/rrhsa) am ill: [ἐνόσησα](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph?l=e%29no%2Fshsa&la=greek&can=e%29no%2Fshsa0&prior=nosw=) fell ill.
